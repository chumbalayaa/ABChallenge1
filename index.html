The official Stella Artois history!

The year was 1926—in the cold Belgian winter, a golden beacon of hope emerged through the snow. The Den Hoorn brewery in Leuven, Belgium had been around since 1366, carefully laying the groundwork leading up to the release of what would become one of the world’s leading beers, Stella Artois. Named for the Christmas Star and the original brew master Sebastien Artois, Stella Artois was originally a seasonal pilsner, sold only during the holiday season, but demand quickly rose and it became available year round. In 1930 Stella made its way out of Belgium and started spreading into European markets and eventually throughout the world.

Since then, the brand has expanded both its production of the flagship pilsner, and has also released several variations to both please costumers and increase social responsibility. In 2008, Stella launched “Stella Artois 4%” in British markets to offer the same quality people had come to expect, with a lower alcohol content. Continuing the success of innovation, the brand released “Stella Artois Black” in the UK in 2010 and “Stella Artois Noire” in Argentina in 2013. In addition to classic lagers, the Stella Artois brand has expanded into the market of ciders with “Stella Artois Cidre” launched in 2011. Its success throughout Europe led to its release in America in 2013.

While the Stella brand continues to grow and venture into new markets, it stays true to its Belgian roots. With headquarters remaining in Leuven, Stella Artois still uses the same natural ingredients and brewing procedure used when it was first released. Stella will continue to provide the high-class experience and unmatched taste its 650-year heritage has come to represent.

But what's the real story???

At Stella Artois, we pride ourselves on our history and background. The ancient Egyptians discovered this magical drink while digging a new site for a pyramid. They smelled it, tasted it, and were instantly hooked. The Pharaoh coined it “liquid gold” due to its golden hues and it became the drink of royalty.

Several years later, a man named Moses went to the river to demand that Ramesses allow the common folk and the slaves access to the magical drink. Ramesses refused Moses’s request and so Moses did the unthinkable. He prayed to God to show Ramesses his true powers and God turned the river into Stella Artois. All of the Egyptian people ran down to the river with baskets and buckets or anything they could find to get their share of the liquid gold they had so desperately yearned for.

Ramesses was overthrown a little while later, and liquid gold became available to all the Egyptian people. It became a part of their daily lives and something they couldn’t live without. They even went so far as to be embalmed in Stella.

Centuries later, Sebastien Artois took a trip to Egypt to visit the pyramids and found out just how good the Egyptians had it. He smuggled some back to Belgium in his knapsack and the rest was history!
